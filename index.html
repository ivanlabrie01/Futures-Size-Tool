05 The three layers

Why two caps beat one

Sizing off the stop alone controls a normal loss. It does nothing about the size you carry across a gap. Layer both.

Layer 1 · normal loss

Risk to the stop

Fix what you lose when a trade fails at its stop — a small, constant slice of equity. This sets size per trade.

size = (equity × risk%) ÷ (mult × stop$)
Layer 2 · worst case

Max size / leverage cap

Independently cap the notional you carry, so a gap through the stop can't erase the account. This is the layer most blowups skip.

notional ≤ cap × equity
Layer 3 · steady heat

Volatility target

Scale down when the instrument is wild, up when it's calm, so risk per day stays even across markets. Caps leverage automatically.

lev = vol_target ÷ instrument_vol
06 The pocket rules
  1. Size from the stop first. Risk a fixed % of equity — 0.5–2% — never a fixed lot count.
  2. Then check leverage. If the position is over your cap, cut it — even if the stop-risk looked fine.
  3. Wilder market → smaller size. Same dollars of daily heat whether it's gold or FX.
  4. Adding margin isn't a fix. More equity buys survival room; it doesn't shrink an oversized position — only closing does.
  5. Changing leverage doesn't change the loss. It changes the stop-out distance. The P&L per point is set by size.
  6. One entry or five — the cap is on total size in that market, not per ticket.
